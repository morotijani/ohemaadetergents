From raw material to your doorstep

Every batch follows the same five-stage line.

No shortcuts between the mixing floor and the bottle in your hand. Here's exactly how a batch of Ohemaa is made.

01 / SOURCE

Raw materials, tested on arrival

Surfactants, fragrance oils, and active ingredients are checked against spec sheets before they reach the mixing floor. A supplier's certificate of analysis is a starting point, not proof — our own lab re-verifies every incoming drum.

02 / FORMULATE

Mixed to a fixed formula

Each product follows a locked recipe by weight, not by eye, so a bottle bought today performs like the one bought last year. Formulas are reviewed annually, but never adjusted mid-batch to cut cost.

03 / QUALITY-TEST

Checked in the lab before bottling

pH, viscosity, and fragrance strength are verified against the standard for that product line. Batches outside tolerance are held and reworked — they don't ship.

04 / BOTTLE & SEAL

Filled, sealed, batch-coded

Every bottle carries a batch code printed at fill time, so any customer issue can be traced back to the exact mix, date, and machine that produced it.

05 / DISTRIBUTE

Out to stockists within 72 hours

From the Kumasi warehouse to shelves across the Ashanti Region and beyond, on a delivery schedule matched to each stockist's sell-through rate.

Standards we hold ourselves to

Quality isn't a single checkpoint — it's four.

Batch-coded traceability

Every bottle can be traced back to its exact mixing date and quality-control result.

72-hour dispatch window

Tested batches leave the warehouse within three days — nothing sits in storage losing freshness.

0.2% rejection threshold

Batches that fall outside formula tolerance are reworked, not sold. It's rare — and that's the point.

Locked, reviewed formulas

Recipes are set by weight and reviewed on a yearly cycle — never adjusted mid-run to save cost.
